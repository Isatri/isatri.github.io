Galaxy Mergers

Modeling galaxy collisions with Python

When we look at galaxies, we see all sorts of shapes and sizes. While most can be categorized as spirals or ellipticals, but the ones that do not fall in these categories, called irregulars, do not have gravitational models that describe them. After some time, we realized that these come from the merging of multiple galaxies.


Typically, modeling galaxy collisions is done on supercomputers with plenty of running time. I had neither a supercomputer nor plenty of time to finish this project, so a few simplifications had to be made.

  • No dark matter
  • No star collisions
  • No star formation
