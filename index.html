Background

Born in the smog-ridden city of Houston, Isaiah's childhood dream of studying the stars seemed worlds away. However, after many trials and tribulations, he was able to enroll in Rice University and pursue a degree in Astrophysics. He currently works with Dr. Andrea Isella studying the origin of asymmetries in protoplanetary disks.

Stellar Interactions with Protoplanetary Disks

My undergraduate senior thesis at Rice University revolved around determining if the asymmetries in protoplanetary disks we see in nature are caused by gravitational interactions with neighboring stars. The Gaia DR2, which was convieniently released after my graduation, is now being used to complete the project.

Searching for Host Stars of Free-Floating Planetary Mass Objects

This on-going project was started during my time in the TAURUS REU program at The University of Texas at Austin. The general idea is to catalog the currently known rogue exoplanets and find potential wide-binary host stars using proper motions.

Creating and Sustaining a Cloud in a Chamber

Ever wonder what clouds do when you're not looking? During my time in the Atmospheric Science REU program at Texas A&M University, I helped in researching the in-cloud production of aqueous secondary organic aerosols. Try saying that one three times fast!

Solar Sodium Lines

One assignment in my Astrophysics I: Sun and Stars class was to develop a code which calculates the number densities for important electron donor elements (neutral and first ionization) in a model solar atmosphere.
