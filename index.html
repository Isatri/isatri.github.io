Background

Born in the smog-ridden city of Houston, Isaiah's lifelong dream of studying the stars seemed worlds away. However, after many trials and tribulations, he was able ot enroll in Rice University to persue a degree in Astrophysics. He currently works with Dr. Andrea Isella in studying the origin of asymmetries in protoplanetary disks.

Origins of Asymmetries in Protoplanetary Disks

This project was started for and used as my senior thesis at Rice University. The basic motivation is determining if asymmetries in protoplanetary disks could be caused by gravitational interactions with neighboring stars.

Finding Host Stars for Free-Floating Planetary Mass Objects

This project was started during my time in the TAURUS program at The University of Texas at Austin. The basic motivation behind the project is to take the currently known rouge exoplanets and find potential wide-binary host stars using proper motion data.
